Predicting Physician Recommendations

This project was a quick exercise building a model that will predict whether physicians are likely to recommended Product_X for any given patient. This is a set of mock data representative of Patient Chart review information provided by physicians.

Based on some basic EDA I decided to use XGBoost for this binary classification task, ultimately acheiving an accuracy of ~85%.

Background

You have been asked to participate in the evaluation of a novel new compound (Product X) being considered for the treatment of "Disease Y". The Medical Affairs team has partnered with leading physicians to complete a detailed chart review of thousands of potential patients. The physicians have documented other relevant treatments and diagnoses each patient has received. Disease Y presents in three types: mild, moderate, and severe. Surgery is an effective treatment of Disease Y, but is highly invasive and results in a significant recovery period.

Instructions

Using the data provided, please build a model to predict whether physicians are likely to recommended Product_X for any given patient. Summarize the data and your findings in a PowerPoint (or equivalent) deck of no more than 5 slides. Your target audience is a mixture of medical, marketing, and analytics leaders, with varying levels of data science familiarity.

Data Detail

"recommend_Product_X" is the target variable

"dx" = "diagnosis

"px" = "procedure or treatment"

"rf" = "Risk Factor" - presence of a risk factor that may or may not influence the appearance and severity of Disease Y
